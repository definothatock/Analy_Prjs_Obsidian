## Purpose

**This page NOT a Standard, but a compact reference table of reusable labels for classifying game attributes during analysis.**

> **IMPORTANT:** 
> These labels are **NOT** analytically equivalent. Some labels are community-used, which can refer to player structure, to mechanics, to progression forms, to presentation, or to broad market categories. They exist to support fast labeling, comparison, and shorthand communication.
> 
> They are not intended to explain nor define systems in depth; Some may also have overlapping tone/definition. These labels should not be a substitute for system description.


*Guidelines*
- Tags on this page are shorthand references, **not complete analyses**
- Prefer precise labels over overly broad or multi-axis labels
- Use as few labels as necessary
- Do not infer full system structure from labels alone
- If a label obscures more than it clarifies, prefer direct written analysis instead

# 1. Player Unit Dynamics
***Defined by actor relations.***
These describe the relationship structure between the player, other players, and game-controlled opposition.

- #Single — primarily solo play; absence of meaningful co-op or PvP structure
- #CoOp — includes meaningful play alongside other players
- #PvP — includes meaningful play against other players
- #PvE — includes meaningful play against game-controlled opposition

# 2. "Genre" Attribute Categories
These are pre-existing label categories commonly treated as “genre,” even though they may describe mechanics, progression structures, presentation, and audience expectation.

>Separate them analytically where possible.

## 2.1 Mechanics Labels
***Units of gameplay interaction defined by rule-bound interaction structures.***

>Mechanics are not limited to the labels below.  
>Define specific framing where needed.

**A. Moment-to-Moment Dynamics (Core Mechanics)**
- Ungrouped
	#Carder — using or deploying cards
	#Platformer — traversing platforms through jumping
	#Clicker — repeated simple inputs for incremental gain
- Shooters
	#FPShooter — shooting and aimed from a first-person viewpoint
	#TPShooter — shooting and aimed from a third-person viewpoint
- Simulations
	#SystemSim — managing or interacting with multiple interdependent systems
	#TaskSim — performing structured tasks that model a specific activity or role
- Strategy
	#RTS — planning and action occur in real time
	#ChessPiece — deliberate positional decision-making with explicit spatial logic


**B. Progression / Loop Modes
- Ungrouped
	#DeckBuilder — constructing or modifying a deck
- Exploration
	#OpenWorld — free-form world exploration
	#Roguelike — repeated runs with heavy variation and meaningful randomness
	#Metroidvania — gated exploration and later ability-based access
- Narrative
	#Walking-sim — navigation, observation, and environmental or narrative discovery
	#VisualNovel — reading, dialogue, and branching or sequential narrative selection


## 2.2 Aesthetic
***Defined by presentation rather than mechanics.***
Labels related to visual style, audio style, tone, rendering style, or atmosphere.


## 2.3 High-Abstraction Genre Labels
***Broad labels applied to the project as a whole rather than to specific systems.***

>Useful for general communication, but often too imprecise for structural analysis. 
>Use mainly when indicating broad community-recognized relations.

Examples:
- 4X
- RPG
- "Friendslop"
