# Listing Game Components


**"Genre"**
- From "game interaction" framing, these are must:
	1. Player unit dynamics
	2. Mechanics

**Player Verbs**
- What are the "Acts" that a player mostly do about.
  Complimentary to Mechanics.
	
	Common example:
	- Jumping
	- Escaping


**Core Resources**
- The Core resources that would Directly change how the player would play/interact/plan.
	
	Common example:
	- Health Points
	- Main trading currency

**Game Verbs**
- what are the common "Acts" that the game/computer would do to the player, and that the player would response to.
  Complimentary to Mechanics.
	
	Common example:
	- Hunt
	- Dropping



***==Optional==***

These are usually hard to know, or less important.

**The Core Rules**
- A unit of logic.
	The underlying "agreements" that shaped the Mechanics and Dynamics.
	
	Common example:
	- Is ...
	- n. should ...
	- If?
	- When?
	etc.
	
	These Rules can be opaqued / hidden from outside view (unlike real games). Might not be as useful as the other sections.

# Independent Discussion

## Game System
***A combination of Mechanics (and Rules), along with highly correlated features. Union of components direct behaviours/outcomes/emotions to land in specific regions (or sets).***
With the above components, what are the Centric Systems?


- Par System Questions
	- What do they transform (intended: in -> out) ?
	- What do they provide (intended: out) ?
	- What do they do (nondeterministic behaviours) ?
	- Why Must this system? What does it provide?
	- How well is it doing? effective and consistent landing in the region? clustered regions?

- Content based questions
	- What are the main Mechanics in this system?
	- What is the role of this Mechanics in the system? Why does it support the intention of the system?
	- 


-  Common Examples:
	- Movement systems
	- Combat system
	- Inventory System


Smaller, less centric systems might also be worth talking, depends on the project talking.
- Examples:
	- Inventory UI Systems
	- Map Systems
